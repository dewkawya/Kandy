Sacred city of kandy

The Sacred City of Kandy is a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Sri Lanka. It was the last capital of the Sinhala kings and is a center for Theravada Buddhism. The city is situated in the midst of hills in the Kandy plateau, which crosses an area of tropical plantations, mainly tea. Kandy is best known for the Temple of the Tooth Relic (Sri Dalada Maligawa), which houses what is believed to be a tooth of the Buddha. The temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is one of the most sacred places of worship in the Buddhist world. Every year, the city holds the Esala Perahera, a colorful and elaborate procession that features elephants, dancers, and drummers.
The city is also home to the Royal Botanic Gardens, which are one of the largest botanical gardens in Asia. The gardens contain a wide variety of plants and flowers from around the world. Kandy is a beautiful and historic city that is a must-see for any visitor to Sri Lanka.
